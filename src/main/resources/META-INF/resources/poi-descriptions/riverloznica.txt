This area includes the wet and marshy meadows along the Ložnica River from Sp. Založ to Zalog in the Savinja Valley. The region, with numerous river meanders, riparian vegetation, flood meadows with moisture-loving vegetation (reed beds, alder groves, sedges), and remnants of lowland oak forests, creates a distinctive landscape and provides a habitat for numerous plant and animal species, including endangered animal species such as: birds - the common buzzard (Buteo buteo), common kestrel (Falco tinnunculus), Eurasian sparrowhawk (Accipiter nisus), grey heron (Ardea cinerea), common quail (Coturnix coturnix), grey-headed woodpecker (Picus canus), red-backed shrike (Lanius collurio), kingfisher (Alcedo atthis), common whitethroat (Sylvia communis), Eurasian nuthatch (Sitta europaea), Eurasian penduline tit (Remiz pendulinus), sedge warbler (Acrocephalus schoenobaenus); amphibians - common frog (Rana temporaria), agile frog (Rana dalmatina), common toad (Bufo bufo), alpine newt (Triturus alpestris), smooth newt (Triturus vulgaris), and green frog (Rana kl. esculenta).
